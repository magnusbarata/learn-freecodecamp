Alan Turing

Father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

About

Alan Turing was a brilliant British mathematician who took a leading role in breaking the German Enigma code, leading to Allied victory over Nazi Germany during WW2. In his seminal 1936 paper, he proved that there cannot exist any universal algorithmic method of determining truth in mathematics, and that mathematics will always contain undecidable propositions.

Turing was highly influential in the development of theoretical computer science, providing a formalisation of the concepts of algorithm and computation with the Turing machine. His work is widely acknowledged as foundational research of computer science and artificial intelligence.

Timeline of Turing's life

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1931 - Enrolled at King's College (University of Cambridge)
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"
  • 1950 - Published "Computing machinery and intelligence"
  • 1954 - Dies at the age of 41

Turing Machine

Alan Turing's work introduced a concept of immense practical significance: the idea of the Universal Turing Machine. The concept of 'the Turing machine' is like that of 'the formula' or 'the equation'; there is an infinity of possible Turing machines, each corresponding to a different 'definite method' or algorithm. But imagine, as Turing did, each particular algorithm written out as a set of instructions in a standard form. Then the work of interpreting the instructions and carrying them out is itself a mechanical process, and so can itself be embodied in a particular Turing machine, namely the Universal Turing machine. A Universal Turing machine can be made do what any other particular Turing machine would do, by supplying it with the standard form describing that Turing machine. One machine, for all possible tasks.

Awards and recognitions

  2. 1994: a road in Manchester city named "Alan Turing Way"
  3. 1999: 100 Most Important People of the 20th century by Time Magazine
